Library

A launcher, not a destination. Projects persist — analysis runs once per track, then every session is open-app → Play.

States

  • Rows show title, BPM · duration, and the saved speed when ≠ 1× — set a speed in the Player and come back to see it stick.
  • Second row is mid-analysis (“Finding beats… 40%”) — tapping it would open the Player in its analyzing state.
  • Third row is a video import: the audio track is being extracted first, then it joins the same pipeline. (Direct YouTube download is a permanent non-goal — save the file, then import.)
  • + opens the system Files picker (audio: MP3/M4A/WAV · video: MP4/MOV). No naming step, no progress modal — it goes straight to the Player.
  • Swipe-to-delete with confirmation; context menu: Rename / Re-analyze / Delete. Duplicate imports are hash-detected (“Already in your library”).
Try: tap the first row to open the Player. Tap + to simulate a fresh import (picker → analyzing → ready).

Player

The app. Giant count, phrase dots, beat-ticked waveform, transport, speed, template — everything auto-saves; there is no Save button anywhere.

States

  • Count display — numeral at ~40% of screen height; “1” is tinted the accent; with the 1 & 2 & template a half-size “&” flashes between numerals. Screen never sleeps while playing.
  • Waveform — accent ticks mark each “1” (downbeat). Scrubbing snaps to the nearest count 1, so every restart is phrase-aligned.
  • ↺ 8 — jump to the top of the current 8-count: the drill-this-phrase button.
  • Speed — pitch-preserving; the voice is never time-stretched, only re-scheduled, so counts stay crisp and locked at 0.5×.
  • Template switch applies at the next “1” so counting never stutters mid-phrase.
  • Controls dim after 4 s of playback without touch; the count and dots never dim.
Try: press Play, then switch to 1 & 2 &, then tap 0.5× — the count slows but never unlocks from the beat. Drag the waveform and watch it snap to a “1”.

Fix Beats · sheet over the Player

Detection will sometimes be wrong — half/double tempo, a shifted grid, or the wrong “1”. Each failure mode has a dedicated one-gesture fix, applied live while the music keeps playing. Verification is playback: the voice against the music is the most sensitive error detector.

Modes

  • Counts A/B — instantly mute/unmute the voice to hear the raw track against your fix.
  • ½× / 2× — the octave-error fix; labels preview the resulting BPM and disable outside 40–220.
  • −10 / +10 ms — phase nudge with live cumulative readout; hold to repeat.
  • Shift 1 ◀▶ — rotate which beat is “1”, deterministic and immune to touch latency. TAP ON 1 re-anchors the phrase to your tap (3+ rhythmic taps also re-fit the phase, latency-compensated).
  • Undo reverts the last correction; Reset restores the detected grid. Done is dismissal — corrections were already live and saved.
  • Bluetooth output shows a delay warning; manual fallback is a two-step flow: tap the tempo until the BPM settles, then tap the “1”.
Try: with playback running, tap 2× and hear/see the count double — then Undo. Tap Shift 1 ▶ and watch the accent “1” move through the phrase.

Big Count · full-screen overlay

For a phone propped across the room: the count at ~70% of the screen’s shorter side, maximum contrast, portrait and landscape. A thin presentation layer over the Player’s state — not a separate screen.

  • Digit pulses subtly on each beat; an accent flash marks every “1”; “&” renders half-size.
  • Phrase dots thick enough to read at 4 m; hairline song progress; current speed badged in the corner.
  • Single tap summons oversized controls (Pause, ↺ 8, speed, Exit) that auto-hide after 4 s; two-finger double-tap always exits.
  • Keep-awake stays on even while paused here.
Try: tap anywhere to reveal the controls; press the big Pause; Exit returns to the Player with state intact.
Demo voice

Speaks the counts using your browser’s TTS so you can hear the concept. It’s robotic and its timing jitters — the real app ships a recorded human voice, sample-locked to the beat by the audio engine.

MVP pillars — what the code must get right

Sync is the product

Voice onsets within ±30 ms of the beat at every speed, zero drift over a full song. One audio clock drives sound and screen.

Detection is fallible by design

Every failure mode (octave, phase, downbeat) has a labeled one-gesture fix, live over playback, persisted per project.

Analyze once

Projects cache analysis locally — reopening any track is instant. Cold launch resumes the last project: open-app → Play, 2 taps.

Audio + video in

MP3/M4A/WAV import, MP4/MOV audio extraction. Export of the mixed file is post-MVP, kept ~100 lines away by the engine design.
